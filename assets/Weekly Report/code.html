Strategic Focus

01. Reports

Earnings Season

Big tech companies are telling us how much money they made. This usually causes big price jumps.

Simple Terms:

Think of this as a school report card for billion-dollar companies.

02. Rates

Fed Divergence

The US Central Bank is deciding how much it costs to borrow money. This affects everyone from home buyers to Apple.

Simple Terms:

The "Fed" is like the market's thermostat. They turn interest rates up to cool things down or down to heat them up.

03. Patterns

Critical Levels

Traders are watching specific price numbers (like 4,200). If the price drops below, things could get fast.

Simple Terms:

Think of these levels like "floor" prices. If the market falls through the floor, it might tumble further.

Capital
Migration

Money is moving. We track where the biggest investors are moving their cash to predict where the next profits might hide.

Analogy: The Rotating Sprinkler

The market is like a garden. Instead of watering everything at once, investors move the "water" (capital) from one area of the garden (like Retail) to another (like Tech) every few months. This is called Sector Rotation.

Pocket Glossary

Bull Market

When market prices are rising or are expected to rise. Named after how a bull strikes up with its horns.

Volatility

How much a price swings up and down. High volatility means prices are jumping around like a wild rollercoaster.

RSI

A speed gauge for stocks. It tells us if people have been buying or selling "too much" lately.

Liquidity

How easy it is to buy or sell something quickly without changing its price. Cash is the most liquid asset.
