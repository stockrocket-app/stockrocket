Module 01 • Introduction

Lesson 1:
What is a Stock?

A foundational guide to understanding ownership, shares, and how the global market functions for beginners.

pie_chart

Ownership via Shares

Think of a company as a giant pizza. A share represents a single slice of that pizza.

When you own a share, you own a literal piece of the company’s physical assets and a claim to its future earnings.

The Golden Rule

"Your ownership is simply the ratio of slices you hold compared to the whole pizza."

rocket_launch

Why do companies do this?

Growth requires massive capital. Instead of borrowing money from a bank (debt), companies sell small pieces of themselves to raise money for new projects and innovation.

  • check_circle Building new factories
  • check_circle Hiring world-class talent
  • check_circle Global infrastructure expansion
payments

How you earn money

1. Dividends

Think of this as a 'thank you' check. Some companies share a portion of their profits with you every few months.

2. Price Growth

As the company becomes more successful, the market value of your 'slice' increases. You can sell it later for more than you paid.

Quick Summary

"A stock is a tiny piece of a company. When you own a stock, you become a part-owner of that business!"
